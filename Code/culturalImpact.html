Cultural Impact

Publicity and Achievement's

The Guardian has praised Minecraft as the best video game of the 21st century to date, and the Polygon has named Minecraft the

"most important game of the decade" in its 2010s "decade in review".
In Forbes best video games of the 2010s list, Minecraft was granted a special mention, saying
"without a doubt one of the most important games of the last ten years."
Minecraft made the World Video Game Hall of Fame in 2020.

Pioneers of the early access business model

One of the first using the model to fund developement before its full release, with the model then becoming commonplace in 2020 indie game development.

Social Media and Minecraft

University of Pennsylvania's Annenberg School of Communication held a study that found a third of Minecraft players learned about the game via Internet videos. Commentator minecraft videos became widespread in 2010, meaning footage of the game with voice-over's were popular. Including player creations, walkthroughs, and parodies. From the 2010s and onwards Minecraft youtube videos gained a multi million number of views accumulated, and in 2014, it was the second-most searched term on the entire platform. By 2018, it was still YouTube's biggest game accross the world.

Minecraft Youtuber Jordan Maron ("CaptainSparklez").

British company The Yogscast has attained billions of views on YouTube uploading minecraft videos. Another well-known YouTuber Jordan Maron, known online as Captain Sparklez, created many Minecraft music parodies over his career, including "Revenge", a parody of Usher's "DJ Got Us Falling in Love". Myoutube until 2019 where it stood in the limelight gaining traction with Youtuber PewDiePie's playthroughs of the game. YouTube later announcing near the end of 2021 that the total amount of Minecraft-related views on the website have exceeded one trillion.

Applications

Minecraft and CAD

Minecraft has been used for computer-aided design (CAD) and education, with the mentioned posibility for the capability to redesign public buildings and parks, which would make it easier to imagine. A member of the Human Dynamics group at the MIT Media Lab, Cody Sumter, said in 2012:

"Notch hasn't just built a game. He's tricked 40 million people into learning to use a CAD program."
Now the software is also out the to allow models such as those in minecraft to be used in 3D printers or personal printers such as MakerBot and RepRap.

The Block By Block Project

In September 2012, Mojang began the Block by Block project with UN Habitat to create real-world environments in Minecraft. Allowing locals to design changes they'd like to see in their environment with minecraft, many areas of concern have been reconstructed. Carl Manneh, Mojang's managing director, called the game

"the perfect tool to facilitate this process", adding "The three-year partnership will support UN-Habitat's Sustainable Urban Development Network to upgrade 300 public spaces by 2016."
Mojang signed Minecraft building community, FyreUK, to help render the environments into Minecraft. The first pilot project began in Kibera, one of Nairobi's informal settlements and is in the planning phase. The Block by Block project is based on an earlier initiative started in October 2011, Mina Kvarter (My Block), which gave young people in Swedish communities a tool to visualize how they wanted to change their part of town. According to Manneh, the project was a helpful way to visualize urban planning ideas without necessarily having a training in architecture. The ideas presented by the citizens were a template for political decisions.

Denmark in fullscale in Minecraft

In 2014, based on their own geodata the Danish Geodata Agency generated the whole of Denmark in Minecraft facilitated by its flat terrain being able to fit in Minecrafts height limit.

The Uncensored Library

Taking advantage of the game's accessibility a non-governmental organization "Reporters Without Borders" created a server "the Uncensored Library", a repository within the game of journalism by authors from countries (including Egypt, Mexico, Russia, Saudi Arabia and Vietnam) who have been censored and arrested. Built by an international team of 24 people in about 250 hours.

Speedrunning

Minecraft speedrunning, where players time themselves beating the game. Some using mods, external programs, debug menus, and others with nothing bu the vanilla game.

Education

Minecraft has also been used for education. In 2011, MinecraftEdu formed to introduce Minecraft into schools. Working to make the game affordable and accessible to schools. The version of Minecraft through MinecraftEdu includes unique features to allow teachers to monitor the students' progress within the virtual world, such as receiving screenshots from students to show completion of a lesson. Near the end of 2012, MinecraftEdu claimed to have reached around 250,000 students through the company. The version of the game with lessons for various subjects such as history, language arts and science. For example one teacher created a large-scale representation of an animal cell within Minecraft that student could explore and learn how cell functions work! Minecraft also has alot of potential within computer-aided design, with the potential to impact the industry.

Redstone

Representing electrical circuits redstone in minecraft has lead to some impressive creations in game such as a functional virtual computers, a working hard drive, an 8-bit virtual computer, and even a smaller-scale version of Minecraft that is playable and able to be built completely in survival mode with no external modifications.

Minecraft and Code.org

The British Museum in London announced plans to recreate its building along with all exhibits in Minecraft in conjunction with members of the public. Microsoft and the non-profit organisation Code.org have also offered Minecraft-based games, puzzles, and tutorials aimed to help teach children how to program; by March 2018, Microsoft and Code.org reported that more than 85 million children have used their tutorials.

Clones

Following the initial surge in popularity in 2010, other video games were criticised for having similarities to Minecraft being called "clones", often due to being built with inspiration from Minecraft, or a superficial similarity. Examples include Ace of Spades, CastleMiner, CraftWorld, FortressCraft, Terraria, BlockWorld 3D, Total Miner, and Luanti (formerly Minetest). Also an adaptation of the alpha version of Minecraft for the Nintendo DS, titled DScraft, has been released; noted for its similarity to the original game considering the technical limitations of the system. In response to Microsoft's acquisition of Mojang and their Minecraft IP, various developers announced further clone titles developed specifically for Nintendo's consoles, as they were the only major platforms not to officially receive Minecraft at the time. These clone titles include UCraft (Nexis Games), Cube Life: Island Survival (Cypronia), Discovery (Noowanda), Battleminer (Wobbly Tooth Games), Cube Creator 3D (Big John Games), and Stone Shire (Finger Gun Games). Though fears were found false when minecraft finally releases on Nintendo consoles.

Minicraft

Markus Persson made another similar game, Minicraft, for a Ludum Dare competition in 2011. In 2025, Persson announced through a poll on his X account that he was considering developing a spiritual successor to Minecraft. He later clarified that he was "100% serious", and that he had "basically announced Minecraft 2".

Oasis

In November 2024, artificial intelligence companies Decart and Etched released Oasis, an artificially generated version of Minecraft, as a proof of concept. Every in-game element is completely AI generated in real time and the model does not store world data, leading to "hallucinations" such as items and blocks appearing that were not there before.

Minecon (Minecraft Live)

Minecon, now known as Minecraft Live, is an annual official fan convention dedicated to Minecraft which usually reveals new games or feature drops, among others. The first full Minecon was held in November 2011 at the Mandalay Bay Hotel and Casino in Las Vegas. The event included the official launch of Minecraft; keynote speeches, including one by Persson; building and costume contests; Minecraft-themed breakout classes; exhibits by leading gaming and Minecraft-related companies; commemorative merchandise; and autograph and picture times with Mojang employees and well-known contributors from the Minecraft community. In 2016, Minecon was held in-person for the last time, with the following years featuring annual livestreams on minecraft.net and YouTube instead.
