Reception

Minecraft has received widespread acclaim from both critics and players, cementing itself as one of the most iconic and influential video games of all time.

Critics

Minecraft was lauded for its creativity and open-ended gameplay. IGN gave the game a 9/10, praising its "beautiful simplicity and endless potential." PC Gamer called it "a brilliant sandbox that encourages exploration and invention." The game's minimalistic art style and freedom to create led to critical success across all platforms.

Sales

As of 2024, Minecraft has sold over 300 million copies, making it the best-selling video game in history. It boasts over 140 million monthly active users. The game is available on nearly every major platform including PC, Xbox, PlayStation, Nintendo Switch, and mobile devices.

Awards

Minecraft has won numerous awards, including:

  • Game Developers Choice Award for Best Debut Game (2011)
  • BAFTA Special Award (2012)
  • Kids' Choice Award for Favorite Video Game (multiple years)
  • Golden Joystick Award – Best Downloadable Game

It is also preserved in the Museum of Modern Art as one of the most significant games in history.

Controversies

While Minecraft itself has remained largely non-controversial, the game’s creator Markus "Notch" Persson faced backlash due to controversial comments on social media after selling Mojang to Microsoft in 2014. Microsoft has since distanced itself from his views. There have also been community moderation concerns in multiplayer servers and third-party content.
