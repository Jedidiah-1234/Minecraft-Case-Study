Development

Editions

Java Edition

The original version of Minecraft, developed in Java for Windows, macOS, and Linux. It allows modding, custom servers, and access to pre-release features via snapshots. Known for its flexibility and active modding community, it is the preferred version for many experienced players.

Pocket Edition

Initially launched for mobile devices, this version allowed Minecraft to be played on the go. It featured touchscreen controls and was a more compact experience. Over time, it was absorbed into the Bedrock Edition family as mobile cross-play became standard.

Bedrock Edition and Console Editions

Built on the Bedrock engine, this edition includes mobile, console (Xbox, PlayStation, Nintendo Switch), and Windows 10/11 platforms. It supports cross-platform multiplayer and has access to the Minecraft Marketplace. The console editions were gradually merged into the Bedrock ecosystem.

Other Versions

There have been niche editions like the Raspberry Pi version designed for education and experimentation, and the New Nintendo 3DS Edition with limited features adapted to its hardware. These versions often served specific audiences or hardware platforms.

Minecraft Education Edition

Designed for classroom use, this version integrates features for collaborative learning and includes tools for teachers to guide students through lessons involving science, history, mathematics, and coding. It has been adopted by schools around the world.

China Edition

Released exclusively in China and operated by NetEase, this edition was free-to-play and localized for the Chinese market. It included region-specific content and operated under separate policies and systems from the global versions.

Minecraft for Windows

This is the Bedrock-based version specifically made for Windows 10 and 11, optimized for performance and compatible with Xbox services. It supports cross-play, the Marketplace, and modern graphics improvements like ray tracing.

Variants

Game Variants

Throughout its development, Minecraft has spawned several variants—some for specific gameplay scenarios, others for experimentation. Variants may differ in their gameplay limitations or expansions, often tied to device capabilities or special purposes like Minecraft Dungeons or Minecraft Earth (now discontinued).

Virtual Reality

Minecraft VR enables players to immerse themselves in the blocky universe like never before. Available on platforms such as Oculus Rift, HTC Vive, and Windows Mixed Reality, it transforms the classic game into a 360° experience with intuitive hand controls and spatial awareness. It brings a new level of tension and wonder to exploring caves, fighting mobs, or just watching the sunset.
